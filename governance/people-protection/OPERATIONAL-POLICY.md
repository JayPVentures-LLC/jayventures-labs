# People Protection Operational Policy

Version: 1.0  
Status: Active

## Purpose

This policy explains how People Protection principles are applied in day-to-day operations, product launches, access routing, partner review, membership systems, and automation.

## Required Review Areas

A People Protection review is required when a change affects:

- Access eligibility.
- Memberships, pricing, perks, or subscriptions.
- Payments, refunds, cancellation, or entitlement routing.
- Discord/community permissions.
- AI-assisted decisions.
- Partner intake or third-party integrations.
- Data collection, retention, analytics, or identity.
- Public trust claims.
- Education, student, or youth-related concepts.
- Evidence releases, public records, audits, or governance reports.

## Decision Standards

A change should pass only when it satisfies all applicable standards:

- The affected person can understand the rule.
- The affected person has a reasonable appeal or escalation path when materially impacted.
- The system does not rely on hidden monetization or coercive access.
- The architecture preserves reasonable portability.
- Partner dependencies are disclosed and mapped.
- Data collection is limited to operational necessity.
- Rollback or remediation is possible.
- The change can be audited later.

## Prohibited Patterns

The following patterns are prohibited:

- Final adverse automated decisions without human review.
- Hidden pricing or intentionally unclear cancellation.
- Forced vendor exclusivity that harms user choice.
- Discriminatory targeting or exclusion.
- Unlawful surveillance.
- Social scoring.
- Biometric mass tracking.
- Child/student monetization through unrelated data use or behavioral advertising.
- Exploitative engagement loops.
- Governance changes that remove appealability, reversibility, transparency, or accountability.

## Required Records

For applicable changes, maintain:

- Commit hash or PR link.
- Reviewer.
- Date.
- Affected system.
- Policy section reviewed.
- Evidence considered.
- Decision.
- Remediation notes, if any.

## Exception Handling

Exceptions must be rare, documented, time-limited, reviewed, and reversible.

No exception may override human dignity, anti-discrimination, anti-exploitation, child/student isolation, appealability, or anti-capture principles.
