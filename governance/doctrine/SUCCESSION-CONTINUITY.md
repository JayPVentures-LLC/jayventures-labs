# Succession and Continuity Doctrine

Version: 1.0  
Status: Active

## Purpose

This doctrine protects ecosystem continuity if leadership changes, accounts are compromised, vendors fail, emergency access is needed, or the founder is unavailable.

## Core Rule

Critical governance, identity, payment, deployment, and evidence systems must have continuity procedures that preserve accountability without creating permanent centralized authority.

## Covered Systems

Continuity procedures should cover:

- Identity and administrator access.
- Break-glass accounts.
- Payment systems.
- Cloudflare deployment.
- GitHub repository protection.
- Microsoft Entra ID.
- Evidence records.
- Public trust documents.
- Partner contact routes.
- Appeal and support routes.

## Continuity Requirements

Critical systems should have:

- Documented owner or steward.
- Backup access route.
- Audit trail.
- Revocation path.
- Emergency contact path.
- Recovery process.
- Separation of duties where feasible.
- Anti-capture review.

## Anti-Capture Rule

Continuity planning may not create permanent unchecked authority. Emergency authority must remain temporary, explainable, auditable, reversible, and accountable.
