# Conflict-of-Interest and Capture Disclosure Standard

Version: 1.0  
Status: Active

## Purpose

This standard prevents governance, review, access, partner, payment, evidence, and escalation decisions from being distorted by undisclosed conflicts.

## Core Rule

Any person or system involved in governance review, partner approval, evidence handling, access restriction, payment decision, institutional escalation, or high-impact AI-assisted review must disclose relevant conflicts of interest.

Conflicted parties may provide evidence but may not be the sole final reviewer.

## Covered Conflicts

Conflicts may include:

- Financial interest.
- Vendor or platform dependency.
- Employment or contractor relationship.
- Family, personal, or close associate relationship.
- Legal exposure.
- Reputation interest.
- Competitive interest.
- Prior involvement in the disputed decision.
- Administrative authority over the affected party.
- Political, institutional, or partner pressure.

## Required Handling

When a conflict exists:

1. Record the conflict.
2. Add a neutral reviewer where possible.
3. Preserve the evidence trail.
4. Prevent the conflicted party from being the sole final decision-maker.
5. Document the final rationale.

## Anti-Capture Alignment

No permanent centralized authority may use conflict, influence, platform dependency, funding pressure, or administrative position to override People Protection principles.
