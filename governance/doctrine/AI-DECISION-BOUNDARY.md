# AI Decision Boundary Doctrine

Version: 1.0  
Status: Active

## Purpose

This doctrine defines what AI systems may and may not do inside the ecosystem.

## Core Rule

AI may summarize, route, detect risk, flag anomalies, draft recommendations, and assist review. AI may not independently finalize denial of access, removal, financial penalty, legal accusation, partner rejection, reputational finding, or other materially adverse decision without human review.

## Allowed AI Support

AI may assist with:

- Summaries.
- Drafts.
- Routing.
- Risk flags.
- Anomaly detection.
- Documentation checks.
- Policy comparison.
- Evidence organization.
- Non-final recommendations.

## Prohibited AI Authority

AI may not independently finalize:

- Account denial.
- Membership removal.
- Payment penalty.
- Refund denial.
- Partner rejection.
- Legal accusation.
- Reputational finding.
- Law enforcement referral.
- Final misconduct determination.
- Child/student eligibility decision.
- High-impact governance decision.

## Human Review

High-impact AI-assisted decisions require:

1. Human reviewer.
2. Reason.
3. Evidence record.
4. Appeal route.
5. Correction path.
6. Conflict-of-interest screening where applicable.

## Explainability

AI-assisted outputs used in high-impact review must be explainable enough for a reviewer to understand what information influenced the recommendation.
