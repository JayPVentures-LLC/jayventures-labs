# Partner Exit and Portability Standard

Version: 1.0  
Status: Active

## Purpose

This standard protects interoperability freedom and prevents vendor lock-in from undermining user, creator, developer, or ecosystem autonomy.

## Core Rule

Every critical vendor dependency must have a documented exit path, export path, replacement option, and operational continuity note.

## Covered Dependencies

This includes identity, hosting, payments, community, AI, analytics, storage, deployment, security, communications, education, and partner workflow systems.

Examples include Microsoft, GitHub, Cloudflare, Stripe, Discord, Wix, OpenAI, Google, Apple, Verizon, HP, Intel, IBM, HashiCorp, and similar services.

## Required Vendor Notes

Critical vendor records should identify:

- Vendor name.
- Purpose.
- Systems touched.
- Data or access involved.
- Exit path.
- Export path.
- Replacement option.
- Operational continuity risk.
- License or terms concern.
- Last review date.

## Prohibited Dependency Patterns

The ecosystem should reject:

- Forced exclusivity.
- Unreasonable exit barriers.
- Hidden data dependency.
- Coercive pricing.
- Non-portable identity, payment, or access paths.
- Vendor terms that erase People Protection obligations.
