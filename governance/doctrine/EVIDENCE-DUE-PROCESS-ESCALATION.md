# Evidence, Due Process, and Escalation Doctrine

Version: 1.0  
Status: Active

## Purpose

This doctrine defines how evidence, review, escalation, and accountability must operate across JPV-OS, JayPVentures LLC, jaypventures, jaypVLabs, JPV Institute, partner workflows, access systems, AI-assisted review, and governance decisions.

## Core Rule

No adverse accountability action may be finalized without evidence classification, reviewer identity, conflict-of-interest screening, decision rationale, appeal path, and preservation of the underlying record.

## Evidence Classification

Evidence should be classified before action is taken.

| Class | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Operational evidence | Logs, tickets, access records, deployment records, payment records | Failed entitlement delivery, deployment audit |
| User-provided evidence | Screenshots, statements, support submissions, dispute records | Appeal submission, billing dispute |
| System evidence | Automated alerts, anomaly detections, AI flags, monitoring output | Abuse signal, fraud anomaly |
| Third-party evidence | Vendor reports, platform notices, legal/regulatory records | Stripe notice, GitHub audit event |
| Public evidence | Public records, published statements, public documents | Public policy statement, official notice |
| High-impact evidence | Evidence that may affect access, reputation, payment, legal escalation, or institutional action | Misconduct finding, malicious manipulation claim |

## Due Process Requirements

A high-impact decision requires:

1. Evidence record.
2. Reviewer identity.
3. Conflict-of-interest check.
4. Decision rationale.
5. Affected system or person identified.
6. Appeal or escalation route.
7. Timestamp.
8. Preservation of the underlying record.
9. Lawful review where external legal or regulatory issues are implicated.

## Escalation Pathways

Issues may escalate through:

- Operational review.
- Security review.
- People Protection review.
- Partner/vendor review.
- Founder or executive review.
- External lawful process, regulator, counsel, court, or appropriate authority where required.

## Non-Retaliation

Submitting evidence, asking for review, requesting appeal, or reporting suspected harm must not result in retaliation.

## Incomplete Evidence

When evidence is incomplete, systems must avoid final adverse conclusions. Temporary containment may be used when there is credible risk, but final accountability requires sufficient review.

## Malicious Manipulation

Policy, law, procedure, or institutional authority may not be used to conceal malicious harm. If misconduct is established through lawful evidence, audit, investigation, due process, or adjudication, accountability must remain available to the fullest lawful extent.
