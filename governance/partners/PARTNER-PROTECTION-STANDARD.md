# Partner Protection Standard

Version: 1.0  
Status: Active

## Purpose

This standard applies to vendors, platforms, sponsors, enterprise partners, creator tools, infrastructure providers, payment providers, identity providers, community systems, research partners, and implementation partners connected to the ecosystem.

## Minimum Standard

A partner or vendor pathway should be approved only when it supports:

- Clear purpose.
- Transparent terms.
- Reasonable exit.
- Data minimization.
- Portability where feasible.
- No hidden monetization.
- No discriminatory targeting.
- No unlawful surveillance.
- No social scoring.
- No exploitative engagement design.
- Human review for material adverse decisions.
- Auditability for high-impact workflows.

## Required Partner Review Questions

1. What system does this partner touch?
2. What data, access, identity, payment, or communication pathway is involved?
3. What user or creator rights could be affected?
4. Is there a reasonable exit or migration path?
5. Are pricing, renewal, cancellation, and refund terms clear?
6. Does the partner require exclusivity?
7. Does the integration create hidden dependency risk?
8. Does the partner introduce automated decisions that materially affect people?
9. Is there a human review or appeal path?
10. Is there a rollback or removal path?

## Vendor-Boundary Notes

Each major integration should include a short note identifying:

- Vendor/service name.
- Purpose.
- Data or access touched.
- Dependency risk.
- Exit path.
- License or terms concern, if any.
- Internal owner/reviewer.
- Last reviewed date.

## Failure Actions

A partner pathway should be paused, redesigned, or rejected when it introduces:

- Hidden data resale.
- Coercive pricing.
- Forced exclusivity.
- Unclear cancellation.
- Discriminatory targeting.
- Unlawful monitoring.
- Non-appealable automated decisions.
- Dependency patterns that undermine portability or governance.
