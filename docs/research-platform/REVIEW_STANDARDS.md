# Research Review Standards

Status: v0.1 operating draft

## Purpose

These standards define how research artifacts are reviewed before being accepted into a framework, specification, measurement system, simulation, dictionary, or atlas.

## Standard 1 — Layer clarity

Every artifact must state where it belongs:

- ontology
- formal language
- mathematics
- measurement
- implementation
- simulation
- validation
- dictionary
- atlas
- archive

Failure condition:

The artifact mixes layers without declaring boundaries.

## Standard 2 — Definition precision

Every key term must have a clear definition.

Failure condition:

A term can be interpreted multiple incompatible ways.

## Standard 3 — Derivability

If the artifact adds a concept, it must show whether the concept can be derived from existing foundations.

Failure condition:

The artifact assumes what it needs to prove.

## Standard 4 — Distinction from existing concepts

The artifact must state how the concept differs from neighboring concepts.

Failure condition:

Two concepts perform the same function and should be merged.

## Standard 5 — Observable evidence

The artifact must identify what would count as evidence.

Failure condition:

No observable evidence can support or weaken the claim.

## Standard 6 — Falsification

Every major claim must include a failure condition.

Failure condition:

The claim cannot be wrong under any observable condition.

## Standard 7 — Measurement pathway

If a concept is intended for empirical use, it must describe how it could be measured.

Failure condition:

No independent evaluator could apply it consistently.

## Standard 8 — Ethical and people-protection review

Artifacts involving humans require explicit boundaries and misuse analysis.

Failure condition:

The artifact could be used to diagnose, accuse, rank, punish, or restrict people without validated evidence and safeguards.

## Standard 9 — Domain respect

Cross-domain translation must not erase domain-specific mechanisms.

Failure condition:

The artifact claims two domains are the same when they only share a function.

## Standard 10 — Revision readiness

The artifact must remain revisable.

Failure condition:

The artifact defends elegance over evidence.

## Review checklist

- Is the layer clear?
- Are terms defined?
- Is the concept derived or justified?
- Is it distinct from neighboring concepts?
- Is evidence specified?
- Is falsification specified?
- Is measurement possible or explicitly deferred?
- Are human-use boundaries present where needed?
- Does it respect domain-specific mechanisms?
- Does it define what would require revision?

## Review outcomes

| Outcome | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Accept | Ready for current target layer. |
| Accept with revisions | Concept is sound but needs changes. |
| Defer | Promising but not ready. |
| Reject | Fails core standards. |
| Archive | Preserve as historical or failed concept. |
