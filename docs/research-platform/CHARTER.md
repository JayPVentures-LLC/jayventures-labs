Status: v0.1 operating draft  
Lane: jaypVLabs research, validation, and experimentation

Classification: research operations framework

## Mission

The research platform exists to preserve, test, refine, and operationalize foundational research artifacts including Adaptive Systems Ontology (ASO), Information Accessibility Theory (IAT), and future companion frameworks.

The platform is not a publication archive only. It is an operating structure for turning emerging ideas into versioned, reviewable, falsifiable, and measurable research.

## Core commitments

### Evidence before expansion

New concepts do not enter a framework because they feel compelling. They enter through documented proposal, review, testing, and revision.

### Ontology before implementation

Universal functions and domain mechanisms remain separate. A concept may belong in the ontology, an implementation domain, a measurement layer, a simulation, or a proposal. Placement matters.

### Stable foundations

Major definitions remain stable inside a major version. Changes require explicit change control.

### Negative results remain visible

Rejected concepts, failed hypotheses, counterexamples, and unresolved contradictions are retained. They are part of the research record.

### People protection

Research involving humans, cognition, intuition, behavior, trauma, identity, or consciousness requires additional safeguards. Frameworks must not be used as standalone tools for diagnosis, accusation, moral ranking, rights restriction, employment decisions, or irreversible decisions about people.

## Research maturity levels

| Level | Name | Meaning |
|---:|---|---|
| 0 | Note | Informal observation or idea. |
| 1 | Proposal | Structured candidate concept or change. |
| 2 | Draft | Documented framework artifact. |
| 3 | Review | Ready for critique against standards. |
| 4 | Experimental | Usable for limited research/testing. |
| 5 | Validated | Supported by defined evidence. |
| 6 | Reference | Stable enough for broader reuse. |

## Operating principle

The platform must preserve identity while adapting to evidence.

If a framework cannot revise itself when evidence demands it, it fails its own adaptive standard.
