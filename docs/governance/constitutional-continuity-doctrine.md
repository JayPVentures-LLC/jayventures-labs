# JPV-OS Constitutional Continuity Doctrine

## Purpose

JPV-OS production governance follows a continuity principle: authority may be executable, but it must remain reviewable, reversible, auditable, and transferable.

This doctrine uses constitutional continuity as a civic model. The 25th Amendment demonstrates that even the highest executive authority is subject to lawful continuity planning, incapacity review, role transfer, and institutional accountability.

## Operating Doctrine

Production authority is a governed privilege, not a default behavior.

Institutional protection is not disloyalty. It is responsibility. Systems that can mutate production, identity, access, payments, communications, or people-protection rules must be bounded by independent review, continuity rules, and operational accountability.

## Infrastructure Mapping

The current enforcement stack supports this doctrine through:

- Protected main branch.
- Required governance, security, and people-protection checks.
- CODEOWNER review.
- Independent approving review.
- Signed commit posture where supported.
- Admin enforcement.
- Strict branch update posture.
- Force-push and branch deletion prevention.
- Conversation resolution requirements.
- Deployment freeze governance.
- Post-merge production gate.

## Continuity Standard

No single actor should unilaterally mutate production systems when the change affects identity, entitlement, payments, communications, or people-protection rules.

Critical authority must remain temporary, explainable, auditable, reversible, transferable, and accountable.

## People-Protection Alignment

Configured security is not sufficient. Governance becomes trustworthy only when enforcement is visible, reviewable, and proven under real operating conditions.

People-protection and continuity are not friction. They are the foundation of resilient infrastructure.
